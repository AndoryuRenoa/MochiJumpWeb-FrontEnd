    What can a web app do?

    Realistically, a web apps can do just about anything. They can live on the internet or live on a local network

    If you have a business with mutliple employees and you want them working on the same system in real time, a web app is an excellent solution. Good examples of areas this is useful are:

  • Sales
  • Customer Service
  • Customer Retention
  • Operations
  • Inventory Managment

    • just to name a few.

    You can have a solution for a specific store or warehouse by keeping the app on a local network, or it can be global by making it live on the internet.

    If you're a one person business and you want to be able to access your business from any computer with an internet connection, then a web app is that solution.

    Web apps can be designed for customer's to interact with, or for employees and owners to interact with. The only limit is imagination.

    Getting a new live web app does not have to replace your current website or require a new domain. The best example I can think of this is gmail living at "mail.google.com." If you'd like a new web app accessable anywhere it can easily be added to a sub-domain.

    Development vs Design

    A developer spends their time working on functionality. A designer spends their time on making things look nice.

    This can be a tricky distinction because there is overlap. The different has more to do with the focus

    As a developer my top priority is designing something to (listed in order of importance):

  • Perform a specific task (or tasks)
  • Be reliable
  • Be easy and fun to use
    • I take pride in that last part, making things easy and fun to use. I take pride in my work, and the user's experience is the rubric that I use to ensure I've developed something that is quality
